Brazil Ransomware Landscape

2021–2026
🇧🇷
BRAZIL — The #1 LATAM Target

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Brazil is the dominant ransomware target in Latin America by every measured metric. The country accounts for 53% of all cybersecurity incidents recorded across the region (SOCRadar LATAM 2026) and 46.94% of all ransomware attacks. ransomware.live tracks 460 cumulative Brazilian victims — the highest in Latin America by a substantial margin. In 2025, 132 confirmed leak-site victims were recorded, with February 2025 setting a national record of over 960 ransomware attacks in a single month.

Brazil entered the global top 10 for ransomware victimization for the first time in February 2026, driven by CL0P's Oracle EBS zero-day mass campaign. The threat landscape is uniquely diverse: global RaaS cartels (Qilin, LockBit, Akira, CL0P) deliberately redirecting operations toward Brazil as a consequence of US and EU law enforcement pressure; Chinese state-sponsored APT groups targeting telecom infrastructure; domestic Brazilian cybercrime syndicates with decade-long operational continuity; and supply chain attacks cascading across the country's large, interconnected healthcare and manufacturing ecosystems.

Crisis & Critical Incident Analysis

Manufacturing alone absorbs 20.56% of attacks (Check Point), and the Qilin-LockBit-DragonForce ransomware cartel alliance formed in September 2025 has specifically concentrated operational resources against the Brazilian market. The CL0P Oracle EBS zero-day campaign (late 2025/early 2026) represented a watershed moment — Brazil's first entry into the global top 10 victim rankings, with exploitation of CVE-2025-61882 across multiple enterprise environments.

Key Threat Groups

Qilin · LockBit · Akira · CL0P · DragonForce · RansomHouse — with the Qilin-LockBit-DragonForce cartel alliance (formed Sep 2025) specifically concentrating operational resources against Brazilian targets.

Analyst Assessment

Brazil's ransomware exposure is structurally driven by five converging factors: the largest economy and digital attack surface in Latin America; a manufacturing and food processing sector running legacy OT systems on complex supply chains; an underfunded healthcare sector with federated IT architecture creating inconsistent security postures; the deliberate redirection of global RaaS operations toward Brazil following US/EU law enforcement pressure; and rapidly expanding cloud adoption outpacing security governance maturity.

// 02 — Latin America

Colombia Ransomware Landscape

2022–2026
🇨🇴
COLOMBIA — Structural Supply Chain Risk

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Colombia has emerged as one of Latin America's most persistently targeted nations for ransomware operations. From the RansomHouse attack on healthcare multinational Keralty in 2022, to the BlackCat/ALPHV breach of energy giant EPM, to the nation-defining IFX Networks supply chain attack of 2023 that paralyzed 50+ government agencies and 762 Latin American organizations simultaneously.

Ransomware.live tracks 128 cumulative Colombian victims. Colombia was the most DDoS-targeted country in Latin America in 2025 (Intel 471). Blind Eagle (TAG-144), a Latin America-based APT cluster active since 2018, continued systematic targeting of Colombian judicial and government institutions through 2025. In April 2026, Qilin claimed the Cooperativa de Hospitales de Antioquia (COHAN). Fintech platform Addi.com was breached by ShinyHunters in May 2026, exposing over 16 million unique records.

Crisis & Critical Incident Analysis

The IFX Networks supply chain attack of 2023 stands as the most significant cyber incident in Latin American history. A single point of failure in IFX's managed services infrastructure cascaded across 762 organizations spanning multiple countries, demonstrating that Colombia's vulnerability is structurally embedded in its centralized IT architecture. The Keralty/RansomHouse incident disrupted healthcare for over 5 million patients. Blind Eagle (TAG-144) represents a persistent nation-state-adjacent threat specifically targeting Colombian sovereignty institutions.

Key Threat Groups

RansomHouse · BlackCat/ALPHV · Blind Eagle (TAG-144) · Qilin · ShinyHunters — with Blind Eagle representing a nation-state-adjacent APT cluster with sustained targeting of Colombian judicial and government institutions since 2018.

Analyst Assessment

Colombia's ransomware exposure is structurally embedded in four key factors: centralized, legacy-heavy government IT architecture; a large underfunded healthcare sector; telecommunications infrastructure reliant on a small number of cloud/hosting providers creating critical supply chain concentration risk; and the persistent presence of Blind Eagle — a nation-state-adjacent APT cluster targeting Colombian sovereignty institutions at a level unmatched elsewhere in the region.

// 03 — Asia-Pacific

India Ransomware Crisis

2022–2026
🇮🇳
INDIA — Structural Proportions

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

India has entered a ransomware crisis of structural proportions. With 455 confirmed cumulative victims tracked by ransomware.live as of June 2026, and 128 confirmed leak-site victims in 2025 alone, India now ranks #1 in the Asia-Pacific region and second globally for ransomware detections, trailing only the United States. Acronis's H2 2025 Cyberthreats Report attributed a staggering 31% of all global ransomware detections to Indian infrastructure.

In January 2026, ransomware velocity against Indian targets surged more than 31% above the prior nine-month average. India topped global charts for lateral movement and mass infection propagation. The primary sectors under siege are Information Technology, BFSI, Manufacturing, and Healthcare — sectors at the core of India's ₹272 lakh crore economy and its $254 billion technology export engine.

Crisis & Critical Incident Analysis

The October 2025 operational alliance between LockBit, Qilin, and DragonForce — sharing infrastructure, tooling, and coordinated targeting — elevated India's threat exposure to historically unprecedented levels. India's rapid digital transformation through the Digital India mission and UPI payment ecosystem has created an attack surface expanding faster than security governance can mature. Cross-border hacktivist groups from Indonesia and Bangladesh have conducted ransomware-adjacent campaigns seeking to undermine India's digital sovereignty. Attackers are specifically targeting IT and BFSI sectors that form the backbone of India's $254 billion technology export industry.

Key Threat Groups

Qilin · Sinobi · TheGentlemen · DragonForce · LockBit 5.0 · CL0P — with the LockBit-Qilin-DragonForce cartel alliance (formed Oct 2025) elevating India's threat exposure to unprecedented levels through shared infrastructure and coordinated targeting.

Analyst Assessment

India represents the world's most rapidly escalating ransomware crisis. The convergence of massive digital attack surface expansion, underinvestment in security governance relative to digitalization speed, the world's largest IT services export economy as a high-value target for data theft and supply chain compromise, and the operational consolidation of the three most aggressive RaaS groups against Indian infrastructure creates a threat environment of global significance requiring immediate structural intervention.

// 04 — Latin America

Mexico Ransomware Landscape

2025–2026
🇲🇽
MEXICO — Structural Shift

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Mexico's ransomware threat environment has entered a critical phase, ascending from 16th place globally in 2024 to 11th in 2025, recording 75 confirmed leak-site victims (Breachsense) against an estimated real-world total of 150–300 incidents. With ransomware.live tracking 248 Mexican victims as of June 2026, Mexico is now firmly the second most targeted nation in Latin America, trailing only Brazil.

LockBit 5.0 breached Sociedad Hipotecaria Federal (SHF), exfiltrating 277 GB of mortgage and financial data; Qilin compromised a second financial institution's electronic transfer channels; and Tengu listed the Junta de Conciliación y Arbitraje as a victim — all within weeks in Q1 2026. The Mexico Ransomware Task Force, launched in March 2026, signals growing recognition that national response capacity requires urgent structural reinforcement.

Crisis & Critical Incident Analysis

The SHF breach by LockBit 5.0 in January 2026 represented a direct attack on Mexico's federal housing finance infrastructure, with 277 GB of mortgage data exfiltrated. The convergence of underfunded public-sector cybersecurity, legacy IT infrastructure in manufacturing and government, supply-chain exposure from nearshoring expansion, and the RaaS affiliate migration following LockBit's partial disruption has made Mexico an increasingly high-value target. The National Cybersecurity Plan 2025–2030 and pending Ley Federal de Ciberseguridad represent progress, but implementation lags behind threat escalation.

Mexico is experiencing a structural shift in its threat environment. The convergence of underfunded public-sector cybersecurity, legacy IT infrastructure, supply-chain exposure from nearshoring expansion, and RaaS affiliate migration has created a rapidly escalating threat trajectory. The Mexico Ransomware Task Force (March 2026) is a positive development, but implementation of the National Cybersecurity Plan 2025–2030 must accelerate significantly to match the pace of threat evolution.

// 05 — South Asia

Pakistan Ransomware Landscape

2022–2026
🇵🇰
PAKISTAN — Structurally Dangerous Phase

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Pakistan's ransomware threat landscape has entered a structurally dangerous phase. Ransomware.live tracks 38 confirmed cumulative Pakistani victims — a figure that dramatically understates true incident volume given endemic non-disclosure. Between January and September 2025, Kaspersky documented over 5.3 million on-device cyberattacks against Pakistani infrastructure, with targeted ransomware detections numbering 42,000. The CEO of PITC disclosed that Pakistan faced 40 million cyberattacks in a single year.

On August 6, 2025, Pakistan Petroleum Limited (PPL) — the nation's second-largest state-owned oil and gas company — suffered a confirmed Blue Locker ransomware attack. In November 2025, the Punjab Forensic Science Agency (PFSA) was claimed by BEAST ransomware with 900 GB of sensitive forensic records. In May 2026, TheGentlemen claimed Beaconhouse School System with over 380,000 students across 8 countries. CL0P's Oracle EBS zero-day swept multiple Pakistani industrial companies in Q4 2025.

The PPL Blue Locker attack (August 2025) targeted the nation's second-largest state-owned oil and gas company supplying over one-fifth of national gas — encrypting systems, deleting backups, and prompting NCERT to issue high-alert advisories to 39 federal ministries. The PFSA/BEAST incident (November 2025) compromised 900 GB of DNA, latent fingerprint, pathology, and computer forensics data for Pakistani law enforcement — a breach with implications for national criminal justice infrastructure. The PPL and PFSA incidents represent attacks on national energy and criminal justice infrastructure respectively — indicating that ransomware is no longer merely an economic threat but a direct challenge to state capacity. Endemic non-disclosure across government and private sectors means the true incident volume likely exceeds reported figures by an order of magnitude.

Peru Ransomware Landscape

2022–2026
🇵🇪
PERU — Escalating National Security Threat

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Peru's ransomware exposure has escalated sharply in 2025–2026, with ransomware.live tracking 69 confirmed cumulative victims as of June 2026 — positioning Peru as the third most targeted nation in Latin America after Brazil and Mexico. The concentration of recent incidents against critical national infrastructure, government digital platforms, financial institutions, and energy companies reveals a threat profile with immediate national security implications.

In October 2024, Interbank — Peru's fourth-largest bank — confirmed a 3.7 TB breach exposing data of over 3 million customers. In May 2025, Rhysida claimed breach of gob.pe, Peru's unified government digital services platform, demanding 5 bitcoin (~$488,000 USD). In November 2025, BlackShrantac claimed SUNAFIL with 200 GB of sensitive regulatory data. In April–May 2026, Peru LNG — the only LNG export facility in South America — was targeted.

Venezuela Cyber Threat Intelligence

2022–2026
🇻🇪
VENEZUELA — Unique Complexity

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Venezuela's cyber threat environment in 2022–2026 is uniquely complex: it combines a growing ransomware-as-a-service victim profile with a parallel layer of destructive, state-adjacent cyber operations against critical energy infrastructure that are unprecedented in Latin America. Ransomware.live tracks 33 confirmed cumulative leak-site victims as of June 2026 — a figure that significantly understates true incident volume given Venezuela's near-total institutional non-disclosure culture.

Banco de Venezuela, the country's largest bank with over 21.7 million accounts, was targeted by LockBit 3.0 in April 2023. In February 2024, Digitel was struck by Medusa ransomware with a $5 million demand. BNC was hit by Qilin in July 2025 with an $8 million demand, then struck again by the same group in April 2026 — a double-victimization unprecedented in Venezuelan ransomware history. PDVSA, the state oil giant, suffered a ransomware attack in December 2025, followed by Lotus Wiper malware targeting the energy sector.

Crisis & Critical Incident Analysis

The Banco de Venezuela/LockBit 3.0 incident (April 2023) targeted the country's largest bank with 21.7 million accounts — one of Latin America's highest-profile ransomware incidents against a state-owned financial institution. The Digitel/Medusa attack (February 2024) carried a $5 million ransom demand against Venezuela's second-largest mobile operator with 4.5 million subscribers. The BNC double-victimization by Qilin (July 2025 and April 2026) — with an $8 million demand in the first attack — represents the first time a single RaaS operator has struck the same Venezuelan organization twice within nine months. The PDVSA ransomware attack (December 2025) and Lotus Wiper malware targeting the energy sector represent destructive state-adjacent operations unprecedented in Latin America.

Key Threat Groups

LockBit 3.0 · Medusa · Qilin · TheGentlemen · Babuk2 · Lotus Wiper — combining criminal ransomware operations with destructive state-adjacent cyber operations against critical energy infrastructure (PDVSA Dec 2025, Lotus Wiper Dec 2025/Apr 2026).

Analyst Assessment

Venezuela represents the most complex cyber threat environment in Latin America. The convergence of criminal ransomware operations (LockBit, Medusa, Qilin) with destructive state-adjacent attacks (Lotus Wiper against PDVSA) creates a dual-layer threat that is unique in the region. The BNC double-victimization by Qilin demonstrates that Venezuelan organizations are being specifically re-targeted. The absence of cybersecurity legislation, near-total institutional non-disclosure, and sanctions-constrained economy create an environment where ransomware thrives in near-total opacity. The true incident volume likely exceeds reported figures by a significant margin.
